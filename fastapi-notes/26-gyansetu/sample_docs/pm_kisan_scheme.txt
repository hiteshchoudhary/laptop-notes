PM-KISAN: PRADHAN MANTRI KISAN SAMMAN NIDHI

Overview:
PM-KISAN is a Central Sector scheme with 100% funding from the Government
of India. It was launched on 24 February 2019 by Prime Minister Narendra Modi
from Gorakhpur, Uttar Pradesh. The scheme provides income support to all
landholding farmer families across the country.

Financial Benefit:
Under the scheme, an amount of Rs. 6000 per year is transferred in three
equal installments of Rs. 2000 each directly into the bank accounts of
eligible farmer families through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) mode.

The three installments are paid in the following periods:
- First installment: April to July (Rs. 2000)
- Second installment: August to November (Rs. 2000)
- Third installment: December to March (Rs. 2000)

Eligibility:
All landholding farmer families are eligible for the scheme, subject to
the following exclusion criteria:

1. Institutional land holders are not eligible.
2. Farmer families in which one or more members belong to the following
   categories are excluded:
   a. Former and present holders of constitutional posts
   b. Former and present Ministers, Members of Parliament, Members of
      State Legislative Assemblies
   c. All serving or retired officers and employees of Central/State
      Government Ministries
   d. All persons who paid Income Tax in the last assessment year
   e. Professionals like Doctors, Engineers, Lawyers, Chartered Accountants
      registered with professional bodies

Registration Process:
Farmers can register for the scheme through:
1. Common Service Centres (CSCs) in their village
2. The PM-KISAN portal at pmkisan.gov.in
3. The PM-KISAN mobile app

Required Documents:
- Aadhaar card (mandatory)
- Bank account details with IFSC code
- Land ownership documents
- Mobile number

Impact and Coverage:
As of 2024, PM-KISAN has benefited over 11 crore (110 million) farmer
families across India. The total amount disbursed exceeds Rs. 2.5 lakh
crore (approximately $30 billion USD).

The scheme has been particularly impactful for small and marginal farmers
who constitute about 86% of all farmers in India. These farmers typically
own less than 2 hectares of land and the Rs. 6000 annual support helps
them purchase seeds, fertilizers, and other agricultural inputs.

eKYC Requirement:
Since 2022, eKYC through Aadhaar-based OTP or biometric authentication
has been made mandatory for all PM-KISAN beneficiaries to continue
receiving installments. This measure was introduced to prevent fraudulent
claims and ensure that benefits reach genuine farmers.

Grievance Redressal:
Beneficiaries can raise grievances through:
- PM-KISAN helpline: 155261 or 011-24300606
- The PM-KISAN portal's grievance section
- Local agriculture office
